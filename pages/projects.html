SupplyLens - Procurement Risk Intelligence System

A pharma manufacturer with no visibility into which of its 15 suppliers represented genuine risk. The question behind the question: how do you triage intervention in a portfolio with no existing risk framework?

I structured a multi-dimensional risk framework across price volatility, delivery reliability, and quality, then built a 4-step agentic pipeline that autonomously classifies supplier risk and generates executive decision memos via LLM. Quarterly reporting went from days to on-demand.

E-commerce · BI May 2026

Beauty & Personal Care - Revenue Leakage Analysis

Revenue was flat despite 18% user growth. The obvious hypothesis was a demand problem. The real answer was a discount strategy problem, and finding that required reframing the business question before touching the data.

I designed a LTV:CAC framework across acquisition channels, discovered that 55% of customers were acquired at a net loss, and quantified ₹1.32 Cr in revenue leakage driven by a 5.5× return rate gap between discount tiers. The output was an automated Power BI dashboard and a budget reallocation recommendation projecting 40–50% CAC reduction.

₹1.32 Cr leakage quantified ₹29.6 L refund risk surfaced 40–50% CAC reduction projected 18 months of channel data

Pharma · Sales Operations April 2026

Pharma Field Force Analytics

The client's problem: zero return on a 20% headcount increase. The real problem: no framework to isolate which part of the field force was underperforming, or why.

I designed the analytical structure from a client brief, building a PostgreSQL star-schema across 200 medical representatives, 500 doctors, and 6 zones. K-Means clustering classified MRs into 4 performance personas, each mapped to a distinct root cause. The synthesis: an 8× conversion gap and ₹45 L revenue opportunity within existing capacity, with zero incremental spend required.

200 MRs analysed 500 doctors segmented 8× conversion gap uncovered ₹45 L opportunity identified
